Inkjet MFPs (Multi-Function Printers)

Key Features

  • All-in-One Functionality: Inkjet MFPs combine printing, scanning, copying, and sometimes faxing in one compact device.
  • Print Quality: Known for high-quality color printing, especially for images and graphics.
  • Affordable: Generally more affordable than laser printers, making them ideal for home and small office use.
  • Versatile Paper Handling: Can print on various media types, including photo paper, labels, and envelopes.
  • Wireless Connectivity: Most models support Wi-Fi, allowing wireless printing from laptops, smartphones, and tablets.

Components and Specifications

  • Print Speed: Typically ranges from 10 to 20 pages per minute (ppm) for black-and-white prints, and 5 to 15 ppm for color prints.
  • Print Resolution: Commonly ranges from 600 x 600 dpi to 4800 x 1200 dpi, providing excellent image quality for color prints.
  • Connectivity: USB, Ethernet, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth connectivity options for flexibility and convenience.
  • Scan Resolution: Usually ranges from 600 x 600 dpi to 1200 x 1200 dpi, delivering clear scans for documents and photos.
  • Paper Handling: Features include automatic document feeders (ADF) and multiple paper trays, with capacities typically between 100 to 250 sheets.
  • Touchscreen Display: User-friendly interfaces with touchscreen displays for easy operation and navigation.

Pros and Cons

Pros Cons

Affordable initial cost, suitable for home and small businesses Slower print speeds compared to laser printers

Wireless connectivity and mobile printing capabilities Lower page yields per cartridge compared to laser printers

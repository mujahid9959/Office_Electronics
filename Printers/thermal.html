Thermal MFPs (Multi-Function Printers)

Key Features

  • Thermal Printing Technology: Thermal MFPs use heat to transfer ink onto paper, offering fast, reliable printing.
  • All-in-One Functionality: Combines printing, scanning, and copying in a single device, making it versatile for various office tasks.
  • Compact Design: Thermal MFPs are generally smaller and more compact, ideal for businesses with limited space.
  • Low Operating Costs: Thermal printers have fewer moving parts, making them more durable and cost-efficient in the long run.
  • High-Speed Printing: Known for producing high-quality prints at faster speeds, particularly in label or receipt printing environments.

Components and Specifications

  • Print Speed: Typically 20 to 50 pages per minute (ppm) for standard models, with faster speeds available in high-end models.
  • Print Resolution: Print resolution generally ranges from 200 x 300 dpi to 600 x 600 dpi, providing clear text and sharp images.
  • Connectivity: Supports USB, Ethernet, and wireless connectivity, making it easy to integrate into office networks.
  • Scan Resolution: Scan resolutions typically range from 600 x 600 dpi to 1200 x 1200 dpi for crisp and accurate scanning of documents.
  • Paper Handling: Thermal MFPs generally support standard A4 or A3 paper sizes, with some models supporting specialty media like labels and receipts.
  • Automatic Document Feeder (ADF): Some models come with an ADF feature to streamline multi-page scanning and copying tasks.

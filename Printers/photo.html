Photo MFPs (Multi-Function Printers)

Key Features

  • High-Quality Photo Printing: Photo MFPs are designed to produce high-quality photo prints with accurate color reproduction and sharp details. Ideal for photographers, photo enthusiasts, and home users.
  • All-in-One Functionality: Includes printing, scanning, and copying, making it a versatile option for users who need to handle both photos and documents.
  • Wide Color Gamut: These printers typically offer a wide color range, providing vibrant, true-to-life photo prints. Some models include dedicated photo ink cartridges for enhanced color accuracy.
  • Wireless and Mobile Printing: Most photo MFPs support wireless connectivity (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth) and mobile printing options, including apps like Apple AirPrint, Google Cloud Print, and more.
  • Multiple Paper Size Support: Photo MFPs often support a variety of photo paper sizes (4x6, 5x7, 8x10, etc.), along with standard document sizes like A4 and letter-size paper.

Components and Specifications

  • Print Resolution: Photo MFPs offer higher print resolutions (usually ranging from 4800 x 1200 dpi to 9600 x 2400 dpi) for photo-quality prints.
  • Ink Type: Many photo MFPs use dye-based ink or pigment-based ink for high-quality photo prints with longer-lasting color.
  • Connectivity Options: These printers generally include wireless (Wi-Fi), USB, Bluetooth, and even memory card slots for convenient printing from various devices.
  • Scanning and Copying: Photo MFPs often come with high-resolution scanning capabilities, with some models offering up to 2400 x 4800 dpi optical resolution for scanning photos and documents.
  • Paper Handling: They support specialized photo paper sizes and glossy papers, in addition to standard paper sizes. Some models have an auto document feeder (ADF) for scanning and copying multiple pages.

Document Cameras

What Are Document Cameras?

Document cameras are electronic devices that project an image of a document, book, or object onto a screen for viewing. They are often used in classrooms, meetings, or presentations to share documents with an audience. Document cameras use a camera to capture the document and display it in real-time on a screen, providing a convenient and effective way to show written or printed materials.

Key Features

  • High-Resolution Capture: Document cameras capture high-quality images, typically in 720p, 1080p, or even 4K resolution, ensuring clear and sharp presentations.
  • Real-Time Display: These cameras allow for the live display of documents, allowing viewers to follow along with real-time annotations and explanations.
  • Flexible Design: Many document cameras feature adjustable arms or stands, providing users the ability to move the camera for optimal viewing angles.
  • Easy Connectivity: Most document cameras can connect to computers, projectors, or interactive whiteboards via USB, HDMI, or wireless connections.
  • Annotation Features: Some document cameras come with built-in annotation software, allowing users to draw on or highlight the documents being displayed.
  • Portability: Many document cameras are designed to be compact and lightweight, making them easy to transport for on-the-go presentations or meetings.

Specifications

  • Resolution: 720p, 1080p, 4K
  • Connectivity: USB, HDMI, Wireless (Wi-Fi or Bluetooth)
  • Capture Area: Varies from A4 to A3 or larger formats
  • Zoom: Optical or digital zoom (varies by model)
  • Built-in Microphone: Some models include a microphone for audio capture
  • Adjustable Arm/Stand: Offers flexible positioning for documents and objects
  • Built-in Annotation: Some models allow real-time drawing or highlighting of documents

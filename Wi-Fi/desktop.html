Desktop Wi-Fi Extender

Key Features

  • Designed for Desktop Use: Larger form factor compared to wall-plug models, often with enhanced cooling and additional features.
  • Wi-Fi Signal Extension: Extends Wi-Fi range, providing a stable connection in areas with weak signal strength.
  • Dual-Band Support: Supports both 2.4GHz and 5GHz bands to ensure maximum performance and flexibility.
  • Easy Setup: Typically features a simple setup process, with some models offering easy-to-use software or mobile app controls.
  • Ethernet Ports: Many desktop Wi-Fi extenders offer one or more Ethernet ports for a wired connection to devices.

Components and Specifications

  • Wi-Fi Standard: Often supports 802.11ac or 802.11n, with some models offering the latest Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax) technology for faster speeds.
  • Speed: Can support speeds ranging from 750 Mbps to 2000 Mbps, depending on the model and Wi-Fi standard.
  • Ethernet Ports: Includes one or more Ethernet ports for connecting devices directly to the extender for improved performance.
  • Power Supply: Desktop Wi-Fi extenders usually come with a dedicated power supply and need to be plugged into a wall outlet or power strip.
  • Compatibility: Works with most Wi-Fi routers and is compatible with various devices like laptops, gaming consoles, and smart TVs.

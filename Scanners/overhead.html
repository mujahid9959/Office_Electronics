Overhead Document Scanners

Key Features

  • Overhead Scanning: Overhead document scanners capture documents from above, making them ideal for scanning large formats, books, and bound documents.
  • High-Resolution Imaging: These scanners offer high-resolution scanning, typically 300 dpi and higher, to ensure clear and sharp images of documents and photos.
  • Versatility: Overhead scanners are designed to scan a wide variety of media, including bound books, fragile documents, or objects that may not be suitable for traditional flatbed scanners.
  • Real-Time Preview: Most overhead scanners come with real-time preview features, allowing users to view the document before final scanning.
  • Ease of Use: Many models are equipped with one-touch scan buttons and software that simplifies scanning tasks.

Specifications

  • Resolution: Typically, overhead scanners support resolutions of 300 dpi to 600 dpi or higher for detailed scans.
  • Scan Area: These scanners often have a larger scan area (up to A3 size or larger), accommodating oversized documents.
  • Scan Speed: Scan speeds for overhead scanners vary, but many offer speeds of 1-3 seconds per page for quick scanning.
  • Connectivity: Overhead scanners can connect via USB, Wi-Fi, or Ethernet, depending on the model and intended use.
  • Software Integration: Many overhead scanners come with software for image enhancement, OCR, and document management.

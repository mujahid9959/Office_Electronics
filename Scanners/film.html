Film Scanners

Key Features

  • High Resolution: Film scanners offer high resolutions up to 6400 DPI, making them perfect for digitizing slides, negatives, and film with high detail.
  • Versatile Compatibility: Supports multiple film formats such as 35mm, 120mm, and medium format, along with slide and negative scanning.
  • Dedicated Software: Comes with dedicated scanning software to optimize image quality, remove dust, and adjust color tones.
  • Color Accuracy: Delivers excellent color accuracy and detail retention, ideal for preserving old film negatives or slides.
  • Digital Output: Scans are outputted in digital formats such as TIFF, JPEG, and PNG, which can easily be edited or archived.

Specifications

  • Resolution: Can go up to 6400 DPI for high-quality scans.
  • Connectivity: Commonly comes with USB or USB 3.0 for fast data transfer.
  • Software: Includes scanning software for adjustments such as dust removal, color correction, and sharpening.
  • Scan Speed: Varies depending on resolution and image size but typically takes 30 seconds to 2 minutes per scan.
  • Film Holder: Includes special holders to scan multiple frames, which can speed up the scanning process.

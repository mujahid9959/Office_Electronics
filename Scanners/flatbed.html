Flatbed Scanners

Key Features

  • High-Quality Scanning: Flatbed scanners are ideal for scanning documents, photos, and books with high resolution, providing clear and detailed images.
  • Versatility: Capable of scanning a wide range of materials, including thick books, delicate photos, and even artwork.
  • Ease of Use: Flatbed scanners are typically easy to use, offering simple buttons for scanning directly to a computer, email, or cloud storage.
  • No Moving Parts: These scanners have a fixed scanning bed and do not require any moving parts during the scanning process, ensuring higher reliability.
  • High Resolution: Many flatbed scanners offer resolutions of up to 2400 x 4800 dpi or higher, ensuring fine details are captured accurately.

Components and Specifications

  • Resolution: Flatbed scanners typically range from 600 x 600 dpi to 2400 x 4800 dpi or higher, providing different scanning quality options.
  • Scan Speed: Scan speeds can vary, with average times ranging from 4 to 10 seconds per page at 300 dpi.
  • Connectivity: Most flatbed scanners feature USB 2.0 or 3.0 connectivity, with some models offering wireless options for added flexibility.
  • Size and Capacity: Depending on the model, flatbed scanners can support a variety of document sizes from letter-size (8.5” x 11”) to larger formats like A3 or legal size (8.5” x 14”).
  • Software Compatibility: Most scanners come with software for image editing, OCR (Optical Character Recognition), and document management.

Shotgun Microphones

What Are Shotgun Microphones?

Shotgun microphones are highly directional microphones designed to capture sound from a specific area while rejecting unwanted noise from the sides and rear. They have a narrow pickup pattern, making them ideal for capturing audio from a distance. Shotgun mics are commonly used in film and television production, theater, sports events, and field recording, where clear audio capture from a distance is necessary.

Key Features

  • Highly Directional Pickup: Designed to capture sound from a specific direction with minimal interference from the surroundings.
  • Narrow Pickup Pattern: Typically offers supercardioid or hypercardioid patterns for precise sound capture.
  • Longer Distance Audio Capture: Perfect for capturing distant sounds or voices while minimizing background noise.
  • Wind Protection: Often comes with accessories like windshields to protect the microphone from wind noise during outdoor use.
  • Applications: Ideal for film sets, live theater, sports broadcasting, and nature recording where isolation of the sound source is crucial.

Specifications

  • Frequency Response: Generally ranges from 40 Hz to 20 kHz, depending on the model.
  • Impedance: Typically between 100 and 200 ohms, providing low to moderate impedance for optimal performance.
  • Polar Pattern: Supercardioid, hypercardioid, or even parabolic in some models.
  • Connector Type: Most shotgun microphones feature XLR connectors, ensuring compatibility with professional audio equipment.
  • Power Requirements: Some models are powered via phantom power (48V), while others may require batteries or operate on USB power for portable use.
  • Application: Best suited for capturing audio at a distance while reducing unwanted ambient noise in noisy environments.

Lavalier (Lapel) Microphones

What Are Lavalier (Lapel) Microphones?

Lavalier microphones, also known as lapel mics, are small clip-on microphones typically worn on the user's clothing, close to the mouth. These microphones are widely used in television, film, public speaking, and presentations due to their discreet design and hands-free functionality. They offer high-quality audio capture while remaining unobtrusive, making them perfect for scenarios where the microphone needs to be hidden or when the user needs to move freely.

Key Features

  • Discreet Design: Compact and clip-on, making them easy to wear without being noticed. Ideal for presentations, interviews, and film productions.
  • Hands-Free Use: Perfect for scenarios where the speaker needs to move freely or use their hands while speaking.
  • Omnidirectional and Unidirectional Options: Lavalier microphones come in both omnidirectional (picking up sound from all directions) and unidirectional (capturing sound primarily from one direction) versions, offering flexibility based on the environment.
  • Versatility: Works well with wireless systems, making them ideal for mobile setups or situations requiring high mobility.
  • High-Quality Audio: Despite their small size, lavalier microphones can capture clear and professional-quality sound, especially in controlled environments.

Specifications

  • Frequency Response: Typically ranges from 20 Hz to 20 kHz, with some higher-end models offering extended range.
  • Impedance: Usually between 100 ohms and 10k ohms, depending on the model and connection type.
  • Polar Pattern: Available in omnidirectional, unidirectional, or cardioid options.
  • Connector Type: Common connectors include 3.5mm jack, XLR, or 1/4-inch plug for professional use. Wireless models often use proprietary connectors or transmitters.
  • Power Requirements: Can operate on battery power in wireless versions or via phantom power when used with wired models in professional audio setups.
  • Application: Widely used for interviews, podcasts, vlogging, presentations, and film production, especially in situations where hands-free operation is required.

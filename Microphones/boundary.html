Boundary Microphones

What Are Boundary Microphones?

Boundary microphones, also known as PZM (Pressure Zone Microphones), are designed to pick up sound from a large area, typically used in conference rooms, auditoriums, or any setting that requires high-quality audio capture from a wider range of sources. These mics work by placing the microphone on a flat surface like a table or floor, using the surface to reflect sound and improve capture quality.

Key Features

  • Wide Pickup Pattern: Boundary microphones capture sound from all directions in a wide area, making them ideal for group discussions, meetings, or conferences.
  • Surface Placement: They are typically placed on a flat surface, such as a conference table, to use the surface as a reflective sound source for enhanced audio capture.
  • Clear Audio: These microphones are designed to provide high-quality audio, even in large or noisy environments.
  • Discreet Design: Many boundary mics are small and unobtrusive, designed to blend into the environment for aesthetic purposes.
  • Wide Application: Ideal for video conferencing, teleconferencing, and public speaking, often used in boardrooms, classrooms, and lecture halls.

Specifications

  • Microphone Type: Condenser or electret, often with a cardioid or omnidirectional polar pattern.
  • Frequency Response: Typically ranges from 50 Hz to 20 kHz for clear, full-range sound.
  • Polar Pattern: Cardioid, omnidirectional, or supercardioid depending on model.
  • Impedance: Usually 200 ohms or higher, ensuring good sound quality and performance in professional environments.
  • Power: Often powered via an external phantom power supply (48V) or battery-operated models.
  • Connectivity: XLR or 3.5mm jack outputs, with some models offering wireless options for added convenience.

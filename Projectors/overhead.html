Overhead Projectors

What Are Overhead Projectors?

Overhead projectors are traditional projection devices used to display transparent images, slides, or documents onto a screen. Typically used in classrooms, offices, and presentations, these projectors have been largely replaced by digital projectors, but are still valued for their simplicity and ease of use in certain applications.

Key Features:

  • Simple Operation: Easy to set up and use, requiring minimal technical knowledge.
  • Transparent Projection: Projects images or text from transparencies onto a screen for large groups.
  • Adjustable Focus: Allows the user to adjust the projection focus for clarity.
  • Portability: Compact and light enough for easy transport, making it ideal for mobile presentations.
  • Durable: Known for their longevity and reliability in basic presentation environments.

Specifications

  • Resolution: Typically lower than modern digital projectors (depends on the transparency being used).
  • Light Source: Uses halogen lamps or other forms of incandescent lighting to project images onto a surface.
  • Lens: Adjustable lens for varying projection sizes.
  • Zoom/Focus: Many models come with zoom and focus controls to adjust the size and clarity of the projection.
  • Power: Requires an electrical outlet to function, often with a long power cord for flexibility in use.

Pros and Cons

Pros Cons

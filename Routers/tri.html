Tri-Band Routers

Key Features

  • Tri Frequency Bands: Operates on three frequency bands â€“ one 2.4GHz band and two 5GHz bands, offering enhanced network management and faster speeds.
  • Reduced Congestion: Distributes traffic across three bands, reducing congestion and providing better performance for multiple devices.
  • Faster Speeds: With two 5GHz bands, users can achieve higher speeds, even when many devices are connected at once.
  • Ideal for High-Demand Activities: Perfect for 4K streaming, online gaming, and large file transfers without compromising performance.
  • Better Range: Tri-band routers provide optimal coverage by balancing the traffic between the three bands, improving overall range and speed.

Components and Specifications

  • Wi-Fi Standards: Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax) and Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac) for higher data transfer speeds and network efficiency.
  • Speed and Coverage: Speeds ranging from 1Gbps to 3Gbps, providing coverage for larger homes or offices with multiple devices.
  • LAN and WAN Ports: Multiple Ethernet ports for wired connections, as well as a WAN port to connect to your modem.
  • MU-MIMO Technology: Multi-user, multiple-input, multiple-output technology allows multiple devices to communicate with the router simultaneously.
  • OFDMA Technology: Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access (OFDMA) allows for better traffic management and improved efficiency, especially in crowded networks.
  • Advanced Security: WPA3 encryption, firewalls, and network security protocols to ensure secure browsing and protection from cyber threats.

Price Range

Price Range: $150 - $500+

Types of Tri-Band Routers

  • Entry-Level Tri-Band Routers: Affordable models suitable for smaller homes or offices with moderate usage and fewer connected devices.
  • Mid-Range Tri-Band Routers: Better performance for larger homes and high-traffic networks, supporting multiple devices simultaneously.
  • High-End Tri-Band Routers: High-speed, Wi-Fi 6 models for large households or office environments with heavy network usage and the need for ultra-fast speeds.

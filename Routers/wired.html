Wired Routers

Key Features

  • Stable and Fast Connections: Wired routers provide reliable and consistent network speeds, ideal for data-heavy applications like gaming, video streaming, and remote work.
  • Secure Networking: Wired connections are more secure than wireless, reducing the risk of unauthorized access or interference.
  • Low Latency: Wired routers offer low-latency connections, making them ideal for online gaming, video conferencing, and high-speed internet browsing.
  • Multiple Ports: Typically come with several Ethernet ports, allowing multiple devices to connect simultaneously for fast, direct network access.
  • Long-Term Reliability: Wired routers tend to have fewer disruptions than wireless alternatives, making them ideal for business or home offices.

Components and Specifications

  • Ethernet Ports: Common models have anywhere from 4 to 8 Ethernet ports for wired connections.
  • LAN & WAN Ports: LAN ports for local network connections and WAN ports for internet connection from the modem.
  • Speed and Bandwidth: Speeds typically range from 100 Mbps to 10 Gbps, depending on the router model.
  • QoS (Quality of Service): Allows prioritizing certain traffic, ensuring smooth performance for specific applications like gaming or video calls.
  • Security Features: Includes WPA3 encryption, firewall protection, and VLAN support for network segmentation and security.

Price Range

Price Range: $30 - $300+

Types of Wired Routers

  • Basic Home Routers: Affordable routers for small home networks with basic features and fewer Ethernet ports.
  • Business Routers: High-performance routers designed for larger offices with enhanced security, multiple ports, and higher speed capabilities.
  • Gaming Routers: Routers built specifically for low-latency gaming, offering features like QoS, customizable settings, and faster speeds.
  • Dual WAN Routers: Routers that support two internet connections, providing redundancy and load balancing for businesses and large households.

Key Use Cases

  • Home Networking: Perfect for families or small households that require reliable internet access for multiple devices.
  • Business Environments: Used in small and medium-sized businesses where stable, high-speed internet is essential.
  • Gaming: Wired routers are preferred by gamers for low-latency and stable connections during online multiplayer games.
  • High-Bandwidth Activities: Ideal for households or offices where activities like video streaming, large file transfers, and remote work are common.

Industrial Routers

Key Features

  • Rugged Design: Built to withstand harsh environments with protection against dust, water, and extreme temperatures.
  • Multiple Connectivity Options: Supports various communication interfaces like Ethernet, Wi-Fi, 4G/5G, and serial ports for seamless connectivity.
  • Reliability: Provides stable and continuous connectivity for critical industrial operations, with redundancy options for failover.
  • Industrial Protocols: Compatible with industrial protocols like Modbus, Profibus, and CAN bus, making it ideal for factory automation and SCADA systems.
  • Remote Management: Many models offer remote management and monitoring features, ensuring quick response to issues and performance tracking.

Components and Specifications

  • Network Interfaces: Ethernet, cellular (4G/5G), Wi-Fi, and serial ports for various industrial network setups.
  • Built-In Security: Includes advanced security features such as VPN support, firewall, and encrypted communication.
  • Power Supply: Wide input voltage range (typically 9-48V DC) to ensure compatibility with industrial power systems.
  • Operating Temperature: Typically ranges from -40°C to 70°C, ensuring operation in extreme conditions.
  • Mounting and Form Factor: Designed for DIN rail or panel mounting for easy installation in industrial control cabinets.

Price Range

Price Range: $200 - $1,500+ (Depending on features and specifications)

Types of Industrial Routers

  • Rugged Ethernet Routers: Designed to provide reliable Ethernet-based connectivity in industrial environments.
  • Cellular Routers: For mobile and remote installations, providing internet access via 4G or 5G cellular networks.
  • Wi-Fi Routers: Provide wireless connectivity in industrial areas, ideal for flexible deployments.
  • Industrial VPN Routers: Specialized for secure remote access, supporting VPN connections for remote monitoring and control.

Key Use Cases

  • Factory Automation: Connects devices in automated production lines, enabling real-time data exchange and remote monitoring.
  • SCADA Systems: Ensures reliable communication between control systems and remote devices in industrial environments.
  • Remote Industrial Locations: Provides connectivity in remote or mobile installations where traditional internet access is not available.
  • Critical Infrastructure: Used in applications where constant and secure connectivity is essential, such as transportation, energy, and utilities.

Stackable Switches

Key Features

  • Scalability: Stackable switches can be easily interconnected to create a larger, unified network, expanding the number of ports available without requiring separate devices for each connection.
  • Redundancy: Provides redundancy by allowing multiple switches to act as a single logical unit, ensuring that if one switch fails, the others continue to function seamlessly.
  • Simplified Management: Stackable switches are managed as one unit, simplifying configuration and maintenance, making them ideal for larger networks.
  • Increased Bandwidth: Stacking switches together increases bandwidth by allowing higher data throughput between switches in the stack.
  • Flexible Deployment: Suitable for environments that require a mix of ports and a flexible, modular design that can scale with the growth of the network.

Components and Specifications

  • Ports: Typically, stackable switches come in 24, 48, or more ports, offering both Gigabit Ethernet and sometimes 10GbE connections for high-speed requirements.
  • Stacking Technology: Switches use either physical cables (like copper or fiber) or virtual stacking protocols to interconnect and function as a unified unit.
  • Stacking Capacity: Can typically support 4 to 8 switches in a stack, depending on the model and manufacturer.
  • Redundant Power Supply: Many stackable switches feature optional redundant power supplies for increased reliability and uptime.
  • Management Features: Offers features like VLAN support, link aggregation, QoS, and SNMP monitoring for advanced network management.

Price Range

Price Range: $200 - $10,000+ (Depending on model and features)

Types of Stackable Switches

  • Unmanaged Stackable Switches: Plug-and-play switches that do not require configuration, ideal for small or simple networks.
  • Smart Stackable Switches: Provides basic management features, such as VLAN configuration and QoS support, without the complexity of fully managed switches.
  • Fully Managed Stackable Switches: Offers advanced features such as SNMP monitoring, VLAN support, link aggregation, and security features, ideal for larger enterprise networks.

Key Use Cases

  • Large Networks: Perfect for businesses and enterprises requiring a flexible, scalable, and redundant network infrastructure.
  • Data Centers: Stackable switches provide the necessary scalability and redundancy to meet the high demands of data centers.
  • Campus Networks: Ideal for educational institutions or large office buildings where network growth and high uptime are priorities.
  • High Availability Networks: Ensures minimal downtime with stackable solutions offering redundancy in case of failure.

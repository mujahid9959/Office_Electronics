Rack-Mount Surge Protectors

Overview

Rack-mount surge protectors are designed specifically for use in server rooms, data centers, and other rack-mounted setups. These surge protectors are built to fit into a standard 19-inch server rack, providing reliable surge protection for equipment housed in a rack. Rack-mount surge protectors help protect sensitive electronic equipment from power surges, spikes, and other electrical disturbances that can cause damage or data loss. They offer a centralized solution for managing multiple devices and ensuring consistent power protection in high-density, critical environments.

Price Range: $50 - $500+

Features

  • Rack-Mountable Design: These surge protectors are specifically designed to be installed into standard 19-inch server racks, ensuring they fit seamlessly into your equipment setup.
  • Multiple Outlets: Rack-mount surge protectors typically feature multiple outlets (6, 8, or more) to accommodate several devices within the rack, offering convenience and organization.
  • High Joule Rating: These surge protectors have a high joule rating, meaning they can absorb larger surges, providing better protection for your sensitive equipment.
  • Energy Efficiency: Many models are designed to be energy-efficient, ensuring that they don’t waste unnecessary power while still providing effective surge protection.
  • LED Status Indicators: Rack-mount surge protectors often come with LED indicator lights to show the status of the unit, such as whether it’s active, grounded, or has experienced a surge.
  • Automatic Shutoff: Some surge protectors include an automatic shutoff feature that disconnects power to the devices when the surge protector has reached its maximum capacity, preventing further damage.
  • Mounting Hardware Included: These surge protectors typically come with all the necessary mounting hardware for easy and secure installation into a rack system.
  • Resettable Circuit Breaker: Many rack-mount surge protectors are equipped with a resettable circuit breaker that can be easily reset after an overload, making them more convenient than traditional fuses.
  • Built-in EMI/RFI Filtering: Some models come with built-in electromagnetic interference (EMI) and radio-frequency interference (RFI) filters to help reduce noise and improve the quality of power to sensitive devices.

Applications

  • Data Centers: Rack-mount surge protectors provide critical protection for server racks and network equipment, helping avoid downtime or data loss caused by power surges.
  • Server Rooms: Essential for protecting servers, routers, switches, and other equipment in server rooms from electrical surges and spikes that could damage sensitive components.
  • Telecommunication Systems: Protect telecommunication equipment, such as routers and switches, that are housed in rack-mounted configurations.
  • Network Infrastructure: These surge protectors safeguard network equipment from damage caused by lightning strikes or power fluctuations, ensuring the reliability of network services.
  • Industrial Equipment: In industrial applications, rack-mount surge protectors can protect equipment like controllers, power supplies, and other machinery connected in a rack-mounted setup.

Conclusion

Rack-mount surge protectors are a critical component in protecting valuable equipment housed in rack-mounted setups, such as data centers, server rooms, and network infrastructure. With prices ranging from $50 for basic models to $500+ for high-capacity, feature-rich surge protectors, they provide an essential safeguard against power surges and spikes that can damage or disrupt sensitive electronics. By ensuring that power to your equipment is clean and stable, rack-mount surge protectors help prevent downtime and maintain the performance of your systems in high-demand environments.

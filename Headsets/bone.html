Bone Conduction Headsets

What Are Bone Conduction Headsets?

Bone conduction headsets are a unique type of headphones that use vibrations to transmit sound through the bones of your skull, bypassing the eardrum entirely. This makes them an excellent option for individuals who need to maintain situational awareness, such as during outdoor activities like running or cycling. They allow users to listen to music or take calls while still hearing surrounding sounds.

Key Features

  • Bone Conduction Technology: Sound is transmitted via vibrations through the bones of the skull to the inner ear, allowing you to hear audio without blocking the eardrum.
  • Open-Ear Listening: The design leaves your ears open, so you can remain aware of your environment, making it perfect for outdoor sports and activities.
  • Comfortable Fit: Most bone conduction headsets are lightweight and comfortable, ideal for long-term use.
  • Waterproof and Sweatproof: Many models come with IP67 or IP68 water resistance, making them perfect for use during workouts or in rainy conditions.
  • Hands-Free Calling: Built-in microphones allow for clear, hands-free phone calls without the need to remove the headset.
  • Bluetooth Connectivity: Many models come with Bluetooth for wireless connection to phones, tablets, and other devices.

Specifications

  • Driver Type: Bone conduction transducers.
  • Bluetooth Version: Bluetooth 5.0 or later, ensuring stable connectivity with mobile devices.
  • Battery Life: 6-8 hours of continuous playback or talk time (varies by model).
  • Water Resistance: IP67 or higher water resistance rating, making them ideal for workouts and outdoor activities.
  • Microphone: Built-in microphone for hands-free calls.
  • Weight: Typically lightweight, ranging from 30-40 grams for most models.
